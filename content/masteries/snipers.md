## 6★ Snipers

### Lemuen

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="4193_lemuen" rows="S3M3,S+,S|S2M3,A+,S-|S1M3,None,None" pool="1" >}}

Lemuen is a powerful sniper in the purest sense of the word. She hits ridiculously hard, and is one of the few Physical damage dealers that can do meaningful damage against pretty much any hard target. She's a great unit worth investment, with two powerful skills, but unlike her sister, there's a lot more overlap between them with less absurd utility (pretty much just her pseudo-global range). While Lemuen is still a strong M6 consideration, you can largely get away with just doing one of them, although the choice isn't as clear as it is with many units.

If you only plan to do one skill, again there isn’t a clear cut answer, but for general use, I would lean towards her S3. You give up some ST ceiling compared to her S2, but you gain a more well rounded skill. She basically charges up, aiming at a target for each ammo, then dumps them all at once. It deals heavy splash damage that allows her to do more than just snipe the big targets, and she doesn't give up a lot of that ST ceiling to do it either. S3 also has significantly higher DPS while S2 can be quite slow in comparison. Mastery on her S3 is nothing too crazy, but DPH is Lemuen's highlight, and it does make a big difference there, so is still a high priority Mastery.

Meanwhile, S2 is focused more on single target sniping. It's better at that task too, which may be what some people want out of her. The nice thing about the skill, in addition to a sky high DPH, is that she only spends her ammo on Wanted targets. She keeps the general skill buffs against the trash, but just shoots them with standard attacks instead. The automatic early fire means less wasted time too. However, it takes a lot longer to get out the same amount of damage even with the early firing. An effective 3.5 second interval to get a maximized shot off makes for a relatively low DPS. Mastery is quite a bit more valuable on S2 though. She gains across multiple aspects of the skill, but gets a notably huge increase at M3. The additional charge potential there adds a whopping +35% more!

To sum up the choice if you can’t afford both, lean towards S3 if you want to maximize your use of her. If you always want her on your team, you’ll get much more use out of it since most maps don’t require her S2’s ceiling. However, if you’re only going to pull her out for those scenarios where you only need that one specific thing absolutely dead, lean towards S2 instead, since S3’s lower ST ceiling could cause problems.

Finally, her S1 may be something to consider for angel maximalists. It's nowhere near as impactful as her other two skills, but as far as AFK skills go, it's a fairly strong one. Her Module effect means it cycles very fast, and it is actually a pretty powerful skill in that niche. But *only* in that niche. For the general player, you give up a huge amount to use it, so should be a fringe consideration only.

### Narantuya

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="4138_narant" rows="S3M3,S,S|S1M3,A-,B|S2M3,B,B" pool="1" >}}

So first of all, Loopshooters can get really complex. There's a lot of detail in Narantuya's kit that I simply can't cover in these writeups (even for as much as I like to yap). The detail is totally not necessary to enjoy her either, but if you're interested in a lot of the mechanical aspects, I would recommend this video by DragonGJY.

That out of the way, Narantuya is (yet another) powerful Sniper. It's a pretty crowded space, but she manages to carve herself out a solid niche thanks to her strong DPS which can flex in a variety of roles as well as her survivability up close that most ranged units lack. While she's not quite the "irreplacable" sort of good, she's a generally solid high tier unit that's very worthy of investment.

As far as Masteries, Narantuya can be a pretty expensive unit since she has three very good skills. However, there is nuance there so that investment will depend on how much you want to use her. The general flow will be her S3, followed by S1 if you want her strong AFK skill, followed by S2 if you want to maximize your use of her. Some players may find her S2 to be more valuable than her S3, but with her upcoming Module¹, it's a relatively clear order now favoring her S3.

Starting with her S3, it generally is stronger up close. That's mainly for reasons related to what S2 does, but S3's triple projectiles when paired with the super short Loopshooter interval up close means a very high DPS. The additional 8-tile damage stacks on even more, and she stacks her Steal much faster too, vital for her up close survability. That up close damage is what Loopshooters are best at, and the Auto Recovery makes it more reliable as well. As we'll get into in a moment, her S2 was a very competitive if not better choice in some cases, but her upcoming Module plays much more strongly for this up close feature. Masteries on S3 are modest, but important thanks mostly to a good-sized improvement to her DPS. The improvement to her cycle is more modest, but still valuable.

After that, most people will probably prefer her S1 rather than a second burst skill. It's a low-SP cost toggle skill, similar to Mountain's S2. It's a good skill with pretty solid DPS against multiple targets. It certainly lacks the ceiling of her other two skills, so this guide favors S3 strongly for that reason, but it's one of the better options in this realm if that sort of playstyle appeals to you.

Last is her S2 which is her AoE skill. It tends to work better at range. This is largely because it expands her AoE coverage. The AoE hits over the entire length of the return at a 1-tile radius, even behind her. That's notable, although not her best use case either. The Attack Recovery on it can be either a blessing or a curse as well since it can seem very short when she can hit targets up close or excruciatingly long at the further ranges it's supposedly better in. That gap in usage to her S3 looked a lot smaller, but with her Module favoring that up close DPS, her S2 looks like a luxury in comparison.

¹ Note Narantuya's Module comes out with the Ch16 patch, expected sometime this spring on global.

### Wiš'adel

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="1035_wisdel" rows="S3M3,S++,None|S2M3,None,None" pool="9" >}}

Wiš'adel is an insane unit. There's no other way to describe it. She is insane even with just E2 but then has ridiculous Mastery gains and a ridiculous Module on top. Everything about her is so ridiculous that she breaks existing ranking systems, including these Masteries grades. She has the highest grade in this guide, but know if there were higher grades available, she would have whatever the top is.

This actually makes Wiš'adel really easy to evaluate in terms of priority. She is the best unit in the game, so you should treat her main skill with the highest priority, and it's so ridiculous that there is little reason to consider a secondary Mastery in most cases! Easy peasy.

Her S3 is that main skill. Don't be scared off by the ammo count, the damage it does is ridiculous on top of a massive splash range with no drop off. As said, the Masteries on it are as ridiculous as the rest of her kit. It is the best skill on the best unit with insane gains. When you're ready to start Masteries, this is the starting point.

Now, setting aside hyperbole, Wiš'adel is actually an interesting case when it comes to secondary skills. Wiš'adel is so ridiculous that even if she didn't have her S3, she would likely still be the best unit in the game. So in an absolute ranking, her S1 and S2 would rank pretty highly. However, for most people, her S3 is just so dominant that there isn’t a lot of reason to justify further investment. It is not often necessary to look beyond her S3.

However, there is one notable case where her secondary Masteries matter, and that is in IS with her S2. There aren't many flaws in Wiš'adel's kit, but if we nitpicked, her S3 has a long cycle and doesn't particularly scale with IS relics. Her S2 though cycles fast and scales insanely with those relics. Considering how pervasive of pick she is (she is easily the best Sniper pick and is usually just straight banned in tournaments), that gives her S2 a lot of value. Now, if you only play on lower difficulties this probably doesn't matter too much. Wiš'adel is so absurd that her S3 will carry you through most of it. However, if you like to push that difficulty, it's a great option on a broken unit, so well worth considering.

Her S1 brings up the rear, although it too is worth acknowledging. However, it will only really find use as an AFK skill or a low-step skill. Some may wonder if it scales in IS too, but it doesn't anywhere near as well as her S2. For a vast majority of people, it’s safe to skip her S1.

### Ray

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="4117_ray" rows="S3M3,S+,S+|S1M3,None,C+|S2M3,None,None" pool="1" >}}

True to her archetype, Ray excels in hunting down single targets. Her main value comes in her incredibly high damage-per-hit (DPH) and strong control, both of which are focused into a single target you really want dead. Her S3 is the key to that value and is one of the best Physical ST skills in the game. Mastery doesn't influence the control and only has a minor impact on the cycle time, but it has a huge impact on Ray's damage. That's vitally important for Ray who is all about her DPH, so her S3 will be something you definitely want to maximize.

In terms of her primary skill, Ray is very straight forward. The nuance to her kit instead comes in with her secondary skills. For most players, it is unnecessary to go beyond her S3. As said, S3 is the core to her meta value and how a vast majority of players will use her. However, the creatively inclined who look to use units beyond their standard niches will find use in both of her other skills.

Her S1 is probably the more potentially useful of the two. Along with her Sand Beast, S1 can Shift enemies at very unusual angles that are usually otherwise impossible. While most certainly situational, it's very fun when it works, and Mastery on it is quite valuable too. S1M3 has dual value in both increasing the force and having a charge immediately ready on deployment, making it particularly important if the gimmick appeals to you.

Meanwhile, her S2 allows for nearly continuous uptime from the Hunter ammo mechanic. It doesn't hit as hard as her S3 which also cycles very fast, but with a well managed Sand Beast, she can virtually continuously fire without falling back in the reloads. That can matter when trying to do speciality clears such as low-operator or niches without blockers. S2 similarly has a valuable M3 breakpoint which drops the Sand Breast's redeployment time, so it's something to consider. However, it's pretty much entirely relegated to niche clears since her already fast cycling S3 will frequently come out ahead with much less effort.

### Typhon

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="2012_typhon" rows="S3M3,S-,A+,Breakpoint - S3M1|S2M3,A+,A" pool="1" >}}

Typhon has one of the more dramatic drops in public opinion between her reveal on CN and her release on global. She came out after a few rather middling banners, and looked pretty amazing then. She had huge range, utility, great damage, and a quality AFK skill to boot! But she came at the forefront of a hefty streak of powercreep, and by her release on global there had been a number of much more powerful banners including a near direct powercreep in Ray, and they'd both get powercrept by Wiš'adel not long after! It all makes Typhon look a lot less impressive than many people will remember.

That bit of history aside, Typhon is still a powerful unit. All of those bits about her range and damage are still true today. There's just a lot of competition now too and her ceiling is quite a bit lower as every new broken banner passes. With that in mind, Typhon is a strong M6, so you'll want to consider both if you plan to use her, but the primary skill to start with will depend on what you want out of her and her other options.

S3 is her big flashy boss killing skill. It has a large effective-DPH (due to the DEF-ignore Talent) on her big range, and can track enemies wherever they might wander or even through phases. It's probably where you'll want to start if Typhon is one of your main damage dealers, whether that's waifu, niche, or just because you're newer. In other words, it will be the best choice if you don't have an alternative boss killing type of skill. It has extremely powerful Masteries on it too. You'll absolutely want to grab the breakpoint at S3M1 immediately, regardless of which skill you ultimately pursue, which adds another arrow to her barrage for a huge damage increase. Further Mastery increases that damage, but also adds another shot per level, so the difference in total damage is pretty dramatic.

However, it is the easier skill to replace as well. If you have plentiful other DPS options, her S2 may be the more appealing skill. This guide typically encourages people away from AFK skills like this, but Typhon has one of the better ones out there. It covers a lot of what her S3 doesn't, and considering her S3 is easier to replace, that comfy big range AFK skill might very well end up more valuable to you. However, Mastery on it also isn't as strong. The increased damage matters, of course, and it's still graded as such, but it's a far less essential improvement than her S3's Masteries.

### Pozëmka

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="4055_bgsnow" rows="S3M3,A+,A|S2M3,C,C+" pool="1" >}}

OGs may remember a time when Pozëmka was largely considered to be EX-tier. It's a testament to how much powercreep we've had that it now seems like an impossibility she was ever that high (a shame too, she's one of my favorites). She's still a pretty solid unit all around and her Typewriter is some really nice utility, but by modern standards neither her DPS or DPH are that special, and her Typewriter isn't as unique anymore either. If you ever wonder how much of my personal bias seeps into this guide, just know how distressed I am to give Pozëmka the grade she has!

All that drama out of the way, Pozëmka is still more than usable, even if she's not exactly special anymore. S3 is still by far her best skill, with solid burst DPS that she can deploy anywhere around the map thanks to her Typewriter. It is restricted to single targets, which is one of its main drawbacks, but does enough DPS to still justify modest priority. Mastery on S3 is fairly valuable too. It's one of those older style Masteries where all aspects of it improve. No one individual improvement is too crazy, but the total effect makes a big difference.

A secondary Mastery is unnecessary with Pozëmka, but if you wanted to do one anyway or are a niche player, her S2 is the one to look at. Manual Activation charge-based skills are generally pretty good, which makes for a good starting point, and the effect along with reduced Typewriter deployment can allow her to spread burst out more than S3 allows. However, a notable frustration is that she can't activate it unless an enemy is in *her* range regardless of the Typewriter. Once upon a time, I had hoped HG would change this, but 3 years and counting later with no such luck... These days, it's a pretty fringe investment.

Pass on her S1 which, aside from the Attack Recovery (which by the way does not charge through her Typewriter), has a too low of DPH to be particularly valuable.

### Fiammetta

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="300_phenxi" rows="S3M3,B,B-|S2M3,B-,B-" pool="1" >}}

Fiammetta is another older unit that hasn't aged terribly well. She's a pure DPS unit, which are always the most apt to get powercrept, and her Vigor + health drain gimmick has always been irritating but is now worse in comparison! However, unlike some of the other 6★ Snipers, she still manages to be decent enough on her own merits. It has meant the general skill suggestion has flipped with her though. S2 was the original suggestion with her, back when burst DPS like it were rare, but the growing power ceiling has meant it's a lot easier to replace, so the unique value of her S3 looks a lot better instead.

So, while not necessarily a meta recommendation anymore, her S3 is still a decent skill that is probably where you'll want to begin Mastery with her. It's certainly her easier skill to understand, and is basically just a continuous barrage of splash damage until you turn it off (get used to the sound effect). While this guide generally avoids recommending AFK-skills like this, Fia's S3 is a reliable source of pretty good splash damage at range with minimal wind-up, which is enough to warrant consideration. Mastery gains on it are decent as well, but unfortunately only the center area damage gets the big impact, so if you dislike it at SL7, further investment probably won't change your mind.

Meanwhile, her S2 used to be her primary suggestion, and probably still should be if you intend to use Fiammetta as an everyday member of your roster. It fires outwards with a slightly delayed explosion after that deals some pretty heavy damage. It's notable that the amount of damage varies quite a bit over her range since certain spots on the path will overlap, so some measure of experience is needed to maximize it. At any rate, it deals quite a bit more damage per activation than her S3, at a less constrained range. Mastery on it is valuable too, dropping her Attack Recovery SP cost by 3, a large amount considering her long attack interval. However, it is "just" DPS without special range, so it will be harder to find work if you aren't dedicated to using her above other options.

Pass on her S1. While it does have a higher DPH, the range is near enough the same as her S3 while also not having special value. It's not bad, exactly, but has a much harder time finding places to be useful than her other skills.

### Fartooth

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="430_fartth" rows="S3M3,None,None" pool="2" >}}

Fartooth. Fart oof. The sounds I make when I read her kit. Oof. Fartooth has the extremely dubious honor of being the first ungraded gacha 6★s in this guide. She wasn't especially good even on her release, but has only aged worse and worse as time has gone on. Her DPS is bad (for a 6★), she has no utility, and her gimmick is near useless, which all makes for a unit where the only good advice is to not bother in the first place. Someone else does everything she does better, and it's not even essential stuff in the first place. She even has a tough time finding room in niches!

If you want to raise her anyway, for whatever reason that may be (I'm not here to judge), her S3 is still the best option, but that's largely due to her other skills being even worse. It still cycles fast and has a decent (but not great) DPH, but the whole gimmick of infinite range but only in a line is really difficult to use. It removes a lot of flexibility that a better range would have, and doesn't give much benefit in return. To make matters worse, the Deadeye Trait means she often wastes shots against random trash that wanders in too. There just aren't many places that sort of range is useful. And if that weren't enough, the Mastery gains on it aren't even especially great!

You should probably avoid her other skills as well. They're tough to use even for waifu purposes. S2 can sometimes be used to cope since it has decent DPS against low-DEF, but it has a problematically low DPH that will only do chip damage against anything meaningful, and a far too harsh of restriction on the global targeting to be useful.

S1 meanwhile is a generic skill. Those are usually skills to avoid, and it's a great mercy to the overall game design that HG stopped including them on 6★s. However, Swift Strike is the least terrible of them, and unlike her S2, it does improve her DPH for when S3's range is untenable. But the fact there's even a positive thing to say about a generic skill here should tell you something about why Fartooth is ungraded in the first place!

### Ch'en the Holungday

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="1013_chen2" rows="S3M3,S-,A+" pool="10" >}}

Ch'en's alter form was perhaps the most controversial character in the game. There were many reasons that go far beyond the scope of this guide, and we don't need to discuss them here, but it's notable how far powercreep has gone that Ch'en the Holungday is no longer considered to be especially top-tier! In any case, while that is definitely true, she's still a powerful DPS unit that's worth fairly high consideration.

Her only skill really worth considering for Mastery is her S3. It's by far her best skill despite a fairly long cycle, since it's the one that integrates most of the things that once made her special. It has high DPH, a large ammo pool for a huge total damage, and pretty solid control. But surprisingly, the Mastery gains on it are actually fairly unimpressive. S3M1 boosts her snare and DEF ignore slightly, but not enough to even warrant a breakpoint label, and she only otherwise gains a tiny amount of SP cost (less than 10%) and a standard amount of damage. That's still worthwhile since you of course want to maximize the damage on your DPS units, but considering Ch'en2's lower place in the meta, it's nowhere near the priority it used to be.

Pass on her other skills unless you're a particular simp for her. While her S3 does have a long cycle, her other skills don't particularly do anything unique that would warrant them as an alternative. If her S3 is untenable for some reason, it's better to just use someone else rather than invest in a secondary skill. There just isn't particular value to be found in either skill. That said, do lean towards her S1 if you really want to do a second. It tends to cycle better for when S3's cycle is too long. It does also get some occasional use in the low-step niche as well, but do note that the Mastery gains on it are again nothing special.

### Ash

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="456_ash" rows="S2M3,B,B+|S3M3,None,None" pool="12" >}}

While most of the Marksman have declined in value due to their low DPH that scales poorly with higher end and recent content, Ash is slightly different. She's always mixed up that a little bit thanks to her S2's higher DPH, and she's even been a pretty solid elite killer at times. However, her shortcoming has always been that it’s tied to the Stun duration of her Talent, effectively restricting Ash’s damage window. While her MAR-X Module does allow her to squeeze most of her ammo into that window, even in a best case scenario it’s 25 seconds down to 6 seconds up, which isn’t great. It’s a short and narrow window to get her damage in. Worse, she doesn’t prioritize her Stun target which can be very troublesome if a different enemy overtakes the Stunned target! In the end it's the same result as the other Marksman. She just doesn't do enough by modern standards to justify a high priority.

But of course, also like most of them, she isn't bad either, so if Ash appeals to you for whatever reason, her Masteries are still reasonable considerations. In such a case, S2 is usually her main skill. It has a reliable on demand Stun and solid DPS as noted above, not to mention her other useful skill has a "per map" limitation which S2 avoids. She has a slightly above average gain to ATK over Mastery too, and a modest gain to her SP cost, which does matter considering her uptime.

If you want more extended use out of Ash, you could consider her S3, although it's a pretty fringe consideration. It does a big burst of damage with a large AoE Shift, both of which can occasionally be useful. However, being limited to twice a map is very limiting considering her off-skill damage is so low. She won't do much except those two breaching rounds. Further, Mastery on it only affects damage, so while her S3 is usable, its Mastery is pretty much only for simps and niche players.

Pass on her S1. Her DPH with it is too low for anything but trash mobs, and even if you think you'd use that, the Mastery gains on it are also really poor.

### Archetto

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="332_archet" rows="S3M3,B,None|S1M3,None,None|S2M3,None,None" pool="2" >}}

* Archetto's IS grades assume her ISW-α Module. This Module is inexpensive, but is given special note here to draw attention to the large gap in value between the modes.

Most of the original Marksman have aged pretty poorly. Their niche of low-DP damage doesn't look as good when everyone has absurd DPS, and their low DPH has always meant a low ceiling, even in their heyday. However, while Exusiai escaped the basement with a busted alt, Archetto escaped it with a solid and fun IS mod. It isn't quite as absurd as some of the other IS mods since her DPH is still low and it constrains team-building, but it's still good enough to give Archetto some decent Mastery consideration.

Fortunately, Archetto's main skill is the same in any case with little value in secondary skills, so the evaluation is pretty easy. The only difference comes down to priority based on if you value IS or not. In either case, her main skill is her S3. Per activation, it has a far bigger impact than either other skill. Of course, that logically makes sense since it has a higher cost. In IS, that high cost is trivial thanks to her ISW Module. The effects mean she generates 2 SP per attack instead of 1 and the expanded range means a higher likelihood of a target being in range. In total, charging up her S3 is trivial, so it makes more sense to go with the bigger impacts.

In the rest of the game, S3 still remains her best skill, but the gap is smaller. In fact, older versions of this guide often recommended S2 as a secondary investment to cover the weakness of her charge time. However, in the modern meta, she simply doesn't have the value to justify that sort of investment anymore when S3 will still be her better skill most of the time.

All that said, S3's Masteries are actually on the weaker side. The biggest gain is to her Attack Recovery cost, but as mentioned, that doesn't matter too much in IS. Beyond that she only gains a much smaller amount of attack compared to other units, so she'll actually work reasonably well at SL7 if resources are tight. However, she's good enough that Mastery shouldn't be completely neglected either!

As for secondary skills, neither is worth much anymore in either mode. If you did want to do one, lean towards S1. It has her highest DPH and a very short charge time at S1M3. The main value over S2 though is the fact you don't have to press the skill button every single time, which is a lot given her charge time in IS! Her S2 meanwhile is largely a gimmick in the current game. The ricocheting arrow can sometimes be nice to have in certain stages, but those are rare, and the charge time is long enough that it will rarely out perform her S3.

### Rosmontis

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="391_rosmon" rows="S2M3,C,None|S3M3,C,None" pool="9" >}}

* Rosmontis's IS grades assume her ISW-α Module. This Module is inexpensive, but is given special note here to draw attention to the large gap in value between the modes. 

Many units have now gotten specialized Modules, but of them, Rosmontis has gotten one of the biggest boosts to her meta value. For a long time now, Rosmontis has been quite a weak unit in general content, and indeed that continues to be true. Her damage is poor, there's flaws on her supposed main skill, and her utility doesn't justify the drawbacks. If you do not plan to use her in the Integrated Strategies mode, continue to treat her Masteries as very low priority across the board. However, her ISW-α Module makes her an invaluable asset, and should for a long time.

Although this is a Mastery guide, it is worth starting with her ISW-α Module effects, since it does influence her skill choice. While all of the effects it provides are strong, as well as being a Flinger which helps by association due to the Flinger Hand Relic, the strongest part of her Module is the Tactical Equipment she summons. This is the same as the major feature of her S3, except the one from the Module is much stronger! Her S2 has always been a strong alternative choice due to its much better cycle and S3's blocking restriction, but now S2 pulls significantly ahead as the skill of choice given her new Module effects. In some cases, her S3 may still be the better skill, depending on what the stage demands, but her S2 now takes the lion's share of the work.

However, all that said, a peculiarity with Rosmontis is that her S2 Masteries have always been notably weak. She is a very strong IS pick, so the investment is still justified, but her Module has by far the bigger impact. If resources are tight and you can only afford her E2, Rosmontis will still function perfectly well, so her grades end up lower when compared to other meta IS picks.

If for some reason you do not care about IS at all and only want to use Rosmontis in regular content, her S3 is usually the better option. However, without that ISW-α boost, she is just not good by modern standards so her expensive Masteries are a low priority. These days, it's best to stick with S2.

### W

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="113_cqbw" rows="S2M3,C,C,Breakpoint - S2M1|S3M1,None,None,Breakpoint - S3M1" pool="9" >}}

Even when she was brand new, I never particularly liked W's kit. It's always been weirdly awkward for the results. While not bad really, no individual aspect is that great, and her damage was unimpressive. Now that we have several years of powercreep and she has an ultra powerful alter, I don't need to hold back! W is a unit that's awkward to use and has aged poorly, so the best advice for Mastery is to look elsewhere. Even niche players will have a tough time finding places to use her in the modern game!

If you want to raise her anyway, for whatever reason that may be, her S2 is probably the only skill still worth Mastery. The RNG placement of her mines can be super frustrating, but they generate fast and she can place them during downtime as well, so the net effect can still be pretty decent. Mastery is valuable too since S2M3 drops 2 SP off of the already low cost, and cycling the mines faster is important for them to be effective. That's probably where you should stop, if you even go that far.

As for her other skills, S3 used to be the suggestion for secondary Mastery, but it's aged really poorly. It still might be depending on what you want out of her since it is her "big boom" skill, but by modern standards the damage is really unimpressive. The time delay means the Stun is hard to place where you want it too. It's just not a good effect anymore, especially for the 6★ costs. However, do note that it does have a valuable breakpoint at S3M1 that adds a new target. That at least will probably be worth grabbing.

Instead, you might consider her S1 for Mastery, although it too is ungraded. It does less total damage, but more per grenade at the impact. More importantly though, the Stun is instant so much more reliable. A 3 second stun for 16 SP isn't exactly good, but it's enough to occasionally be useful if you're really intent on using her.

### Rosa

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="197_poca" rows="S3M3,B+ *,B+ *|S2M3,B+ *,B *" pool="2" >}}

* Note that Rosa's skills are graded assuming she has her SIE-X Module at level 3. If you do not plan to get this Module fully upgraded, treat the priority of these skills significantly lower.

Even by modern standards, Rosa's DPS is pretty decent thanks to how generally valuable the Besieger Modules are. So despite her age as a unit, she's still worth considering. However, she isn't a special tier either and her cycles are a notably awkward part of her kit compared to the ever growing competition. Rosa can be a pretty expensive unit to use properly too. She has two good skills which are both relatively necessary due to those awkward cycles, as well as a needed fully upgraded Module, so don't expect great returns if you're going to be cheap!

The typical starting point for Masteries with her is S3. While it has low uptime compared to her downtime, the impact while it's up is quite potent. The per second damage results in a pretty good burst, and the Bind for the entire duration is one of the longer effects of its type. While that uptime can be a problem, it cycles fast to compensate. Mastery on it is fairly valuable too thanks to an extra target at S3M2 and an extra second of duration at S3M3, although the damage gain itself is nothing special.

Her S2 is her secondary Mastery. The cycle time on it is awkward as both the long uptime and long downtime mean it rarely lines up with other more common skill cycles, but that big uptime does look very nice when her S3's short uptime is a problem. It's notable that S3 actually has higher DPS, even against fairly high DEF, but since the uptimes are so drastically different, S2 will often deal much more total damage. Mastery on it is valuable too thanks to a 15 SP drop in her already high downtime in addition to the extra damage. It's not a particularly amazing skill, and doesn't have any special meta value, but it's solid enough to warrant Mastery if you plan to actually use Rosa.

Pass on her S1 which is just a generic ATK up skill with little upside. Thankfully, these are a thing of the past, although I'm sure some bear maximalist would find some occasional use in its different cycle.

## 5★ Snipers

### Skybox

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="4213_skybx" rows="S2M3,B,B|S1M3,C,C" pool="4" >}}

Skybox is our first Skybreaker, a minor riff on the existing AoE Sniper archetype, Artilleryman, you're likely already used to. They trade some range and DP cost for a better interval, and only do splash damage on skill activation, but most notably and true to their name, they fly, only having to land during their skill durations. This allows them to avoid a lot of damage sources, which means they can be pretty aggressively placed, as long as you're careful with things. This makes Skybox a relatively decent unit for his rarity, although still quite off meta, especially in the modern era. However, if you like off-meta play or just think he's neat, he also has some pretty valuable Masteries.

While both of his skills have some value, and there's a good chance you'll want to do both if you choose to raise him in the first place, his S2 tends to be his main skill. It's our first Overload skill in a long long time, and basically means that during his last 5 shots he gets an extra effect. This leads to an interesting dilemma in some situations. Do you end his skill early for no gain so he takes back off to relative safety? Or do you leave him down long enough to reach that Overload state for the final big burst? In either case, that controllable nature gives it a significant advantage over his S1, on top of better damage and range.

S2 also has some shockingly valuable Masteries, especially considering his rarity. The ATK increase is pretty straightforward, but is an above average amount, resulting in a 33% DPS and total damage improvement, the latter of which is especially nice with his Overload burst. However, he also gets a good sized SP cost improvement, dropping by 5 SP, and a large increase to his initial SP, dropping from 14 SP to just 5.

Meanwhile, his S1 is useful, but a bit less impressive. Considering his long interval, the overall damage difference from his S2 actually isn't that large, and the improved splash area can even make it come out ahead. The gains over Mastery are quite good too, although not as strong as S2. The damage increase is "just" 25%, although that's still well above average, and the SP reduction at S1M3 doesn't matter quite as much as skills of this type typically are given the higher base SP cost. However, the biggest flaw with the skill is that it's uncontrollable and with a much shorter range, which leaves him much more vulnerable than his S2 does. With S2, you can have him take back off to safety whenever needed, or even just out range them, whereas with S1, he'll land and potentially get shot at no matter what.

Note that on his release, there was a bug with his S1 where his landing state would prevent the SP from being consumed and allow him to spam it. You may see various highlight clips of it still. This bug was fixed however, and should not factor into your decision.

Finally, while his Mastery gains are strong, it's notable that he is still functional at lower investment values. While these are big improvements, his damage at base is pretty decent, as is his SP cost, so Mastery on either skill isn't exactly required as it sometimes is with high gain skills. His E2 promotion is notably poor too which adds to the effective cost, so if you just want to give him a basic E1 to try him out, you'll be fine.

### Snow Hunter

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="4211_snhunt" rows="S2M3,B,B+" pool="1" >}}

Snow Hunter is a rather nice addition to the 5★ Sniper lineup. She does good damage thanks to a triple hit (two from S2 plus one from Talent), and can precisely control it thanks to S2's short cooldown, Manual Activation, and Charge-based nature, which is almost always a potent combination. She even gets a nice bit of on-demand control too since she can stack her two activations to Freeze her target. However, she is still a 5★ so while these features are nice, they don't measure up to the ever exploding meta. Her damage is nice, but not amazing, and she is stuck on ST. This means she isn't a general consideration for most people, but she is reasonably solid for those who favor off-meta approaches.

S2 is her main skill, both because it has all of the features that make her good and because her other skill is a stinker. S2 has significantly better damage and that Charge-based Manual Activation nature that is oh so good. It's also her only skill that can Freeze since S1 otherwise cycles too slow, and even better, it doesn't use the Hunter's ammo mechanic, meaning the archetype's drawback doesn't hamper her output much. Mastery on it is extremely valuable as well. The damage increase at base is already around a 25% improvement, and even higher versus stationary targets, but the SP cost also drops dramatically which makes a big difference on skills like this.

In comparison, her S1 is... frustrating. Generic Power Strike skills like this are generally poor design in the first place. With only a few exceptions, they're basically filler for a skill slot that HG couldn't be bothered with. But Power Strike is especially perplexing on a Hunter like Snow Hunter (boy that sounds weird to say). Even setting aside the damage difference, the Hunter's ammo mechanic means the Attack Recovery will charge very slowly against consistent pressure. She also loses the benefit of her S2's special shot, so even the strike itself is reliant on the ammo. Then she also loses her ability to Freeze since the Cold debuff will always wear off before a second layer can be applied. It's just a very perplexing inclusion when most design lately has been pretty darn good.

However, S1 might have some niche use in IS if you really love using both her and Archetto. Despite the flaws and lazy design of the skill, when paired with Archetto's IS Module, it actually has stronger DPS than her S2, even if forced to reload constantly. However, without Archetto, it is much worse, and she also loses her Freeze ability unless also paired with Gnosis. Also keep in mind Gnosis benefits both of her skills, so Gnosis' IS Module doesn't give her S1 an edge. While the combo with Archetto makes her S1 usable in IS, you're almost certainly better off using someone else in non-niche situations.

### Brigid

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="4177_brigid" rows="S2M3,B-,B-" pool="4" >}}

Loopshooters are a fairly strong archetype thanks to their solid DPS and survivability at a relatively low DP cost. Brigid continues that trend, although as the middle-child of the group, doesn’t do much to differentiate herself. She strikes many of the same notes as her higher rarity counterpart, Narantuya, without doing anything new or different, and what she brings over her lower rarity counterpart, Caper, is minor. It all means that Brigid isn’t a bad unit, but also probably not one that’s worth the investment outside of niche or desire considerations. If you have Narantuya, she has no place on the team, and if you don’t have Narantuya, Caper is the more cost effective option.

If either niche or desire applies to you, both of her skills have potential value, but the skill to start with is her S2. It does really good DPS and the attached Slow keeps her target in that close high-DPS range that’s so vital for a Loopshooter’s DPS. Unfortunately, it can be hard to place where you want it since it’s a single target only, which means the current target will often get overtaken by someone else. It also can never be a 100% uptime Slow since it only applies while “attached” and ends immediately when the projectile returns. So it’s a solid effect, but still a limited one, and the Offensive Recovery SP cost can be daunting. Further still, the Mastery gains on it are pretty weak, as is her E2 promotion. So if you find she’s unimpressive at E1 SL7, further promotion probably won’t change that opinion.

If you are really dedicated to using Brigid, then her S1 may be worth considering as well, although it is the weaker of her skills. It can be more effective against multiple enemies and doesn’t have that troublesome SP cost. However, it has a weak DPH and loses that control value her S2 has, so it’s a waifu Mastery only.

### Firewatch

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="158_milu" rows="S1M3,A,A|S2M1,None,None,Breakpoint - S2M1" pool="2" >}}

Firewatch is one of the rare 5★s that's managed to maintain some value the higher rarities can't quite do (yet). Like April, this is stealth based, but Firewatch is a bit different. Invisibility is actually more powerful since it will block splash and AoE damage too, which hurts Camo'd units! Not too bad for a launch unit in a pretty weak rarity.

Of course, as with April, that isn't something that can't be replaced by all of the absurd meta-defining DPS out there. Firewatch is hardly a must-raise sort of unit, but she is still a useful tool to have when you're starting to look outside of the peak of the meta. If that's you, S1 is her main skill since it has that aforementioned Invisibility. Mastery on it is absurdly powerful too, and it doesn't really have that mentioned absurd uptime until S1M3. The difference is actually staggering, going from a typical 26 down / 35 up to an unheard of 20 down / 50 up, and that difference is vital for the dangerous places she's typically placed in!

If you're interested in getting more out of her, you could consider her S2 as well, but it's a much more fringe Mastery. If you play in a niche it can sometimes be useful for dropping a quick burst of damage, but the cycle time is the polar opposite of her S1, so she'll spend a lot of time doing not much of anything. However, you should probably at least grab the S2M1 breakpoint which adds another bomb. Even that might not ever matter if you only want her stealth, but it doesn't cost much either.

### Meteorite

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="219_meteo" rows="S2M3,B,B|S1M3,B-,C" pool="2" >}}

### Coldshot

{{< mastery-table-enhanced id="4104_coldst" rows="S2M3,None,None" pool="4" >}}
